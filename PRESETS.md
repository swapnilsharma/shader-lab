# Frakt Presets

A field guide to the twelve built-in scenes. Each preset is a starting point — drop one in, then push the sliders.

---

## Agate

**Feel:** A polished slice of stone held up to the light.

Deep teals and pale ice-blues swirl through layered fBm noise. Polar twist pulls everything toward a center, chromatic aberration fans the edges into faint rainbows, and a soft bloom catches the highlights. It's the most "produced" of the presets — eight stacked effects working together.

**Motion:** Slow, geological. The flow-warp drifts at 0.52, but the bloom and vignette make it feel still even when it isn't.

---

## Aurora

**Feel:** Northern lights you don't have to fly to see.

Six-stop mesh gradient over near-black: deep navy → teal → mint → spring green → violet → back to black. Two layers, mesh + color-grade. Saturated but never shouty.

**Motion:** Brisk and confident. Speed 1.36, large soft bands sweeping across the canvas like curtains.

---

## Cosmos

**Feel:** Looking up from the bottom of a well at midnight.

Cobalt → indigo → violet → blue, ringed by deep space black. Adds film grain on top so it doesn't feel CG-clean.

**Motion:** Steady, planetary. Speed 1.1, slow rotation through the palette — closer to "sky" than "animation."

---

## Ember

**Feel:** Watching a hot coal breathe.

Maroon → blood orange → pure orange → pale gold, anchored in near-black. The most warm-spectrum preset. Saturation pushed to 1.2.

**Motion:** Soft and breathing. Speed 0.9, large blobs drift slowly — the way embers actually pulse, not how movies show fire.

---

## Holo

**Feel:** Iridescent sticker under fluorescent light.

Pastel cycle: ice blue → lilac → pink → cream → mint → ice blue. The only preset on a *light* canvas (off-white). Subtle chromatic aberration and grain pull it back from looking like a CSS gradient.

**Motion:** Quick. Speed 1.3, the whole rainbow loops without a clear "start."

---

## Liquid Prism

**Feel:** Black-and-white photograph that someone tinted by mistake.

Five-layer post-processing chain on a near-monochrome gradient (charcoal → mid-grey → soft white). The hue shift in color-grade (340°) tints the whole thing rose-pink. Bloom and chromatic aberration do the heavy lifting.

**Motion:** Almost still. The slow gradient makes the bloom highlights drift like sunlight across a wall.

---

## Lux

**Feel:** Stage spotlight on dark velvet.

Layered: warm-white glow over a black-and-grey gradient, with a deep purple gradient (`#7c27c2` → near-black) overlaid at 11% opacity. Heavy bloom (strength 1.62), light grain, soft vignette.

**Motion:** Quiet. Most of the visual life comes from the bloom blooming, not the underlying noise.

---

## Minimal

**Feel:** Default desktop wallpaper for someone with taste.

Five greys, smooth gradient, light grain. That's the whole thing. No hue, no bloom, no chromatic aberration — the restraint is the point.

**Motion:** Brisk (speed 1.8), but you barely notice because there's nothing to compare against.

---

## Plasma

**Feel:** A 90s screensaver, but tasteful.

Pure saturated cycle: hot pink → yellow → cyan → violet → hot pink. Tiny chromatic aberration adds a glow at the edges. Two layers, no grain, no grade — clean.

**Motion:** Very slow (speed 0.22). The colors are loud; the motion is patient. That contrast is the whole effect.

---

## Silk

**Feel:** Velvet draped across a dressing table.

Plum → magenta → pink → cream gradient, dusted with grain. The palette runs from deep wine to nearly white, so it has both depth and shine.

**Motion:** Almost imperceptible. Speed 0.08 — the grain is what your eye registers as movement.

---

## Static

**Feel:** A CRT with bad reception, made deliberate.

Forest green / lime palette warped by both flow-warp and noise-warp, then sliced by scanlines. Grain on top. A scene built around imperfection.

**Motion:** Active and noisy. Flow speed 1.23 plus noise drift plus scrolling scanlines — the most kinetic preset.

---

## Sunrise

**Feel:** The five minutes before the sun clears the horizon.

Cream → peach → coral → magenta → indigo. Light canvas, warm bias. The only preset that genuinely looks "warm" without being orange.

**Motion:** Glacial. Speed 0.06 — slow enough that two screenshots a minute apart look identical, but stand and watch and the bands shift.
